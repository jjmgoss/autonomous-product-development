# Product Skill: Competitor and Substitute Analysis

## Goal

Evaluate whether existing tools, workarounds, or incumbents already solve the problem well enough.

## When to use

- during research synthesis
- during opportunity selection
- during validation

## Procedure

1. List direct competitors.
   - products explicitly built for the same workflow
2. List substitutes.
   - spreadsheets
   - scripts
   - internal workflows
   - general-purpose tools
   - service providers
3. Ask why users still use them.
   - familiarity
   - acceptable quality
   - low switching cost
   - integration depth
   - trust
4. Identify where they fail.
   - missing workflow depth
   - poor UX for the niche
   - slow manual work
   - weak visibility or quality controls
5. Judge whether the current answer is already good enough.
   - if yes, the opportunity may be weak even if complaints exist
6. Record the credible wedge for displacing or complementing the substitute.
7. Cite saved evidence IDs for the most important substitute claims.

## Output expectations

- named competitors and substitutes
- why they persist
- why they are insufficient for the target niche
- why switching might still be hard

## Common failure modes

- analyzing only venture-backed competitors and ignoring spreadsheets or manual workflows
- assuming user complaints mean users will switch
- treating a slightly cleaner UX as a sufficient moat
