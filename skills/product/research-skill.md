# Product Skill: Research

Use this skill when exploring a theme for real product opportunities.

## Goal

Find repeated user pain, broken workflows, unmet needs, and meaningful dissatisfaction in the target area.

## Procedure

1. Identify likely user groups within the theme.
2. Collect evidence from public discussions, reviews, issue trackers, forums, social posts, or community writeups.
3. Separate recurring pain from one-off complaints.
4. Record what users are currently doing instead.
5. Distinguish high-frequency low-severity pain from lower-frequency but high-severity pain.
6. Capture candidate opportunities in `docs/research.md`.

## Output expectations

For each meaningful pain point, record:

- who experiences it
- what the workflow is
- what goes wrong
- how often it appears to happen
- why existing tools are insufficient
- what a narrow product response might be

## Anti-patterns

- inventing user pain from personal intuition alone
- treating broad dissatisfaction as a coherent opportunity
- jumping to solution ideas before the problem is clearly framed
- relying on one flattering source
