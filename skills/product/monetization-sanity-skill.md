# Product Skill: Monetization Sanity

## Goal

Determine whether an opportunity is merely interesting, genuinely useful, or plausibly the seed of a monetizable digital product.

## When to use

- during opportunity selection
- during validation
- before making pricing or business claims

## Procedure

1. Identify the economic reason the user might pay.
   - time saved
   - revenue unlocked
   - risk reduced
   - quality improved
   - recurring operational friction removed
2. Identify plausible pricing shapes.
   - subscription
   - seat-based
   - usage-based
   - recurring report or monitoring
   - one-time setup only if recurring expansion is still believable
3. Check willingness-to-pay signals.
   - current spending
   - current tool costs
   - paid workarounds
   - expensive manual effort
   - urgency tied to deadlines or quality failures
4. Check whether value recurs.
   - recurring workflows are stronger than one-off novelty
5. Check sales simplicity.
   - prefer products that can be adopted by a small team or individual without a long enterprise process
6. Record what would make the idea commercially weak even if it is technically buildable.

## Questions to answer

- Why would this user pay instead of tolerate the current workaround?
- What budget or measurable value is adjacent to the pain?
- Is the likely price high enough to matter and low enough to feel credible?
- Would this still be valuable next month, not just the first time?

## Common failure modes

- calling an idea monetizable because SaaS exists as a category
- confusing user interest with willingness to pay
- ignoring the gap between a useful tool and a viable business seed
